The Methodology

Six Factors.
One Score.

01

Location Type

Remote and rural areas have fewer wired options, making Starlink's satellite coverage far more valuable.

02

Existing Access

If fiber is already at your door, Starlink rarely wins on speed or price. No service at all flips the equation.

03

Local Providers

We score your available ISPs on reliability, speed, and value — then stack them against Starlink's $120/mo baseline.

04

Budget

Starlink costs $120–$250/mo. If budget-conscious providers can meet your needs, they score higher here.

05

Primary Use

Gaming and latency-sensitive work penalize Starlink's 20–60ms average. Casual browsing and streaming are less affected.

06

Household Size

More users mean more simultaneous demand. Starlink's shared capacity can struggle with large households.
